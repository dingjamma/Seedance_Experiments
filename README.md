# 🎬 Seedance Experiments

Personal experiments with **ByteDance's Seedance 2.0** video generation model via **即梦AI (Jimeng)**.

Inspired by Hollywood-level AI video generation. Currently failing to recreate it. Documenting everything anyway.

---

## 🎯 The Goal

I want to see how close a regular person with no film production background can get to cinematic-quality AI video — think battle sequences, dramatic lighting, character animation, lip sync. The kind of stuff that used to take a studio and a $200M budget.

Spoiler: it's harder than it looks.

## 🛠️ Platform

- **Model:** Seedance 2.0
- **Interface:** 即梦AI / Jimeng (China) — paid membership
- **Prompt language:** Chinese (Mandarin) — required for best results on the platform
- **Reference images:** Up to 4 slots (@1–@4), used to anchor character appearance and scene background

---

## 📖 What I'm Learning

- Chinese video gen platforms have aggressive IP moderation — named characters from major franchises get blocked instantly
- The bypass is to describe visually what the reference image shows, not name who it is
- Seedance 2.0 is solid for single-character, low-motion clips; multi-character action sequences are at the edge of its capability
- Prompting for video is a completely different skill from image prompting — timing, camera language, and motion description matter enormously
- Hollywood quality is not happening anytime soon from a single text box
